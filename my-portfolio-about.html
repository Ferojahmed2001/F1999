About me

I’m a bioinformatician and data analyst with a passion for leveraging data to solve complex biological and healthcare challenges. My expertise lies in using statistical analysis, machine learning,and computational models to extract meaningful insights from large-scale datasets.

With a background in statistics, I’ve worked on projects ranging from genome sequencing and molecular biology to predictive modeling and data-driven decision-making in healthcare. I have strong programming skills in Python, R, and SQL, and am experienced with key tools like scikit-learn, and various bioinformatics platforms.

My data science work includes designing and implementing machine learning models, performing advanced statistical analyses, and visualizing data to drive actionable insights. I am particularly interested in fields like personalized medicine, drug discovery, and population genomics where the intersection of biology and data science can create groundbreaking innovations.

Whether working independently or as part of a multidisciplinary team, I thrive on tackling complex datasets and finding patterns that contribute to a deeper understanding of biological systems. I also enjoy collaborating with others to develop solutions that can positively impact healthcare and research.

When I'm not analyzing data or coding, I enjoy learning new things and solving puzzles, which help keep my creative and problem-solving skills sharp. Feel free to explore my portfolio to see some of the projects I’ve worked on and how I can contribute to your next endeavor.
